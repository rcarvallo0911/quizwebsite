WHAT IS A CONSPIRACY THEORY?



Over the decades, conspiracy theories have become more popular in our society. These conspiracy theories are mixed with facts and over imagination. Conspiracy theories are theories that suggest that one secretive group controls and causes events and/or other situations. However, not everyone believes in these conspiracy theories. Usually believers are the uneducated and unstable financially. Although there are many different conspiracy theories in all topics that can affect everyone differently. Believers can be anyone, it is really based on the mentality of the person and the situation. There are some people who are more prone to believing in conspiracy theories. These people lack education like a graduate degree, probably are young or not very involved in society therefore lack experience, and/or are overall very anxious or paranoid person. Since believers/conspiriests can be anyone, there are many different types of conspiracy theories. Here we will talk about two of many popular conspiracy theories.

Mandela Effect


Nelson Mandela supposed death in prison was the source of this theory. Named after him because many people were certain he died in prision during the 1980s, but ended up actually dying in 2013. The Mandela Effect is a theory that attempts to explain the phenomenon in which a large part of society remembers a different scenario or outcome of the past. There are many variations of this theory, but the main one is that someone from the future is going back in time and making small changes in the orginal past. A situation like this is rare as most Mandela Effect instances are actually minor or insignificant qualties of everday things. An example of this is the Mona Lisa. Most people believed that orginally the Mona Lisa was not smiling, but the current painting shows she is.
